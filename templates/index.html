🌬️ Wind Velocity & Direction Analysis
Why Wind Matters for DLR

Wind provides convective cooling to the overhead conductor. Higher wind speed increases heat dissipation, allowing more current to flow safely — raising the DLR ampacity.

However, the cooling effect also depends on wind direction relative to the conductor axis. Perpendicular wind provides maximum cooling; parallel wind provides minimum cooling (IEEE Std 738-2012, Eq. 4).

This study focuses on the Ayer Tawar–Pantai Remis 275 kV transmission line in Manjung, Perak, Malaysia. The critical section was identified by Tenaga Nasional Berhad (TNB) as having potential risk of violating the required 7-metre ground clearance limit when conductor temperature approaches the maximum allowable limit of 75°C.

The Dynamic Line Rating system monitors real-time weather and operating conditions to provide a more accurate ampacity estimate than the fixed Static Line Rating, helping operators avoid thermal overload while maximising grid transfer capacity — especially under heatwave conditions.
